The second-generation Audi Q7 was first released for the 2016 model year, and is still in production today, albeit with some upgrades here and there. As a luxury vehicle, it’s important to stay on top of maintenance for your Audi Q7. By servicing your Audi Q7 at regular service intervals, either once a year or every 10,000 miles, you can keep it running and driving for years to come. If you are unsure of your last service, you can schedule a mobile service today and we can determine which maintenance items are needed for your Audi Q7.

Below we have outlined the Audi-recommended services for the second-generation Audi Q7 (2016-Present). You can use this Audi Q7 maintenance schedule to determine which services are needed for your particular Audi Q7.

Audi Q7 Service Intervals

10,000 Mile Service

  • Oil Change – Replace engine oil and filter.
  • Inspection – Check brake pads, tires, and fluid levels.

20,000 Mile Service

  • Cabin Air Filter – Replace with new filter.
  • Plenum Panel – Check drainage system and clean as necessary.
  • Sunroof – Clean guide rails and re-grease to ensure function.
  • Brake Fluid – Inspect and change if over 2 years old.
  • Oil Change – Replace engine oil and filter.
  • Inspection – Check brake pads, tires, and fluid levels.

30,000 Mile Service

  • Oil Change – Replace engine oil and filter.
  • Inspection – Check brake pads, tires, and fluid levels.

40,000 Mile Service

  • Cabin Air Filter – Replace with new filter.
  • Plenum Panel – Check drainage system and clean as necessary.
  • Sunroof – Clean guide rails and re-grease to ensure function.
  • Brake Fluid – Inspect and change if over 2 years old.
  • Oil Change – Replace engine oil and filter.
  • Inspection – Check brake pads, tires, and fluid levels.

50,000 Mile Service

  • Oil Change – Replace engine oil and filter.
  • Inspection – Check brake pads, tires, and fluid levels.

60,000 Mile Service

  • Engine Air Filter – Clean housing and replace filter.
  • Spark Plugs – Inspect and replace all spark plugs.
  • Drive Belt – Replace compressor belt.
  • Cabin Air Filter – Replace with new filter.
  • Plenum Panel – Check drainage system and clean as necessary.
  • Sunroof – Clean guide rails and re-grease to ensure function.
  • Brake Fluid – Inspect and change if over 2 years old.
  • Oil Change – Replace engine oil and filter.
  • Inspection – Check brake pads, tires, and fluid levels.

70,000 Miles and Above:

Starting at 70,000 miles, you can expect these to continue at regular intervals, including oil changes every 10k miles or one year, cabin air filters every 20k miles, spark plug and transmission fluid changes every 40k miles, and brake fluid replacements every two years, regardless of mileage.
